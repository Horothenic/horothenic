### **MEET CATARINA**

On **March 1, 2025**, our first baby was born — a daughter named **Catarina**.

I have loved the name **Catherine** since I was young. It is my favorite female name. We regionalized it to Spanish and Brazilian — **Catarina** — and the coincidence with my wife's story made it even better: she is Brazilian, born and raised in the state of **Santa Catarina**.

She was born tiny — **2.730 kg** and **49 cm** — just like us. I am tall but slim, my wife is small and slim, and Catarina came out matching the family recipe perfectly.

She is the **first granddaughter on both sides of the family**, so she is cherished a lot. And she deserves every bit of it.

![Catarina](/img/posts/catarina-sleeping.png "Catarina")

### **THE DAY SHE WAS BORN**

Catarina was born at **Hospital Real San Jose**. Thanks to my job's insurance, we did not pay anything — and they also gave me **4 months of parental leave**.

Labor started around **midnight**, but my wife thought they were farts. Classic.

At **9 AM** the pain got real. By **midday** we went to see the doctor, and from there straight to the hospital.

Fun fact: my wife's mother had arrived from Brazil **just two days earlier**. Perfect timing.

It was a **natural** birth. After we got to the room, my wife stood there for about **15 minutes** — and then we went straight to the labor room. Less than an hour later, Catarina was here.

A small, cute little potato.

My wife tried to have her while standing, but it was too hard for her. Fair enough — she still got her out, and that is what matters.

### **BECOMING A DAD**

I have wanted to be a dad since I was **20 years old**. This is one of my greatest achievements.

It took me a long time to realize I was actually a dad. Not in theory — I knew she was mine — but in that deep, surreal way. My best memory so far is **having her at the hospital**. It felt unreal. Joyful. Like life had quietly shifted into something bigger.

Catarina slept pretty well. We never suffered too much from lack of sleep — though **my wife definitely got the worse of it**.

Funny enough, I balanced life quite well. I started waking up at **5 AM** to play **Tibia** before the baby or my wife woke up. Life is good.

![Catarina at the desk](/img/posts/catarina-desk.png "Catarina at the desk")

### **CONCLUSION**

We made a human. A tiny one. A loved one.

Welcome to the world, Catarina — and welcome to the blog, little potato.
