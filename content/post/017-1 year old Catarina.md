One year old, celebrated in Brazil, and already running the house.
<!--more-->

### **ONE YEAR**

This year has been **awesome**. We have had a couple of problems — minor, really — but Catarina has been a good girl. She **sleeps well at night**, and at the time I am writing this *(June 2026)* she is the best. Such a happy girl.

She walked about **three weeks before her birthday** and started talking around the same time. Now she walks like a pro, repeats whatever we say, and already obeys some instructions. We are very proud.

![Catarina first birthday](/img/posts/catarina-birthday-1.png "Catarina first birthday")

### **BIRTHDAY IN BRAZIL**

We celebrated **exactly on March 1st** in Brazil, with my wife's family — in **Santa Catarina**, **Florianópolis**.

We actually celebrated **twice**. First with the African side of the family in **Porto Alegre**, and then on her birthday in Florianópolis with the **Italian side**.

Two families, two cities, one very loved one-year-old.

### **THAT FLIGHT WAS HELL**

The flights were a mess.

The **three planes to Brazil** were good — Catarina slept most of the time. No complaints there.

The way back to Mexico was a different story. On the longest leg, **9 hours**, she slept **one hour**. One. That was hard.

I am not excited about flying again when she is **3 years old**. We made a pact: **Brazil every 2 years**. That feels right.

### **MAMAÉ, PAPAI, AND UVA**

She started with **mamaé** and **papai** in Portuguese. Now her favorite words are the foods she likes — **uva**, **tortilla**, **agua**.

She started walking slowly, and we got to watch it happen step by step. **Two steps** turned into three, four, five... and then the entire room. And beyond.

She looks very intelligent — or at least **quickly adaptable**. I tend to try new things with her every week and she succeeds. I can tell her to close doors or drawers. If I point, she walks in that direction. She gives me things and receives things. And she can tell you if she wants something.

That something tends to be **mom**. No questions asked — my wife is her favorite person. Maybe we will have problems later breaking that obsession. She cries sometimes when she cannot find her, even when I am hugging her.

Fair enough. I would probably do the same.

### **CONCLUSION**

One year of Catarina. Brazil, family, milestones, and one brutal flight home.

We usually avoid posting photos of her to protect her privacy — this one is from the side, no frontal face, and it felt right for the occasion.

Happy first birthday, little potato. Year two, here we come.
